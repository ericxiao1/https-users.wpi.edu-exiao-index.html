Violin

Violin is an instrument I have played since I was about 8 years old. Before I started playing the violin, I played the piano. However, the piano wasn’t an option for an instrument in my middle school (Sherwood), so my parents wanted me to start playing a new instrument, so I could get a head-start. My mom recommended me to choose the violin, and she told me that it was the hardest instrument to play, so I decided to take on a hard challenge.

In 2018, I started taking lessons from a violin teacher who used to teach one of my parents friends son. I learned how to play the instrument quickly, and I was able to playing simple pieces and was able to play in tune. However, bowing properly and holding the bow correctly were both challenges for me. Even though I was able to play all the notes correctly, and in tune, the sound quality would always ruin my performances. My teacher told me that it didn’t matter if I played all the notes correctly if my tone sounded squeaky.

Knowing that I would need to improve my tone quality, I tried some ways to fix it. I would sometimes try to play from memory, and would focus on if I was bowing straight or play in a mirror. Eventually, I was able to fix most of my bad habits with bowing. My teacher recommended me to try out for the district auditions. She told me that I would have a good chance of making the cut, and that it would be beneficial for me in the future if I did.

The song for the audition was a simple song, and a song I had played before. Just like my teacher predicted, I did make the cut, so I would have to commit two online orchestra concerts. I felt very achieved making it in the Junior Districts but I knew there was still some skills to work on.

In conclusion, the violin has been a main hobby for me, and definitely a challenging instrument to play.
